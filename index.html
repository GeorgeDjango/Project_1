About me


Hi there! My name is George Django Parker, and I am a Worcester based music tutor specialising in Bass, Guitar, Drums and Ukulele.

Growing up in Spain, I started a band called "The Hoaxx" at age 15 with my close friends Daniel Whybrow, Ben Davis and Sebastian Baldwin. We began gigging most weekends and I considered myself to have become a working musician at the age of 16, picking up many useful skills along the way. After a couple of years "The Hoaxx" ended up parting ways with Ben Davies and became a three piece meaning that I had to begin filling out the bands sound on bass with more melodic and interesting basslines and begin singing backing vocals.

At age 20 I moved back to the UK and studied music at universtiy, alongside my bandmates. Unfortunately "The Hoaxx" ended up parting ways as our music tastes had changed over the years. At this point I auditioned and joined a new originals alternative rock band called "Underground Ocean", solidifying my music theory and studio skills.

"Underground Ocean" released a handful of singles, an EP, a couple of music videos and toured across the UK working with many up and coming bands. As time went on the a couple of members of the band decided they needed a break from touring and gigging, and the band took a hiatus.

After working in "Underground Ocean" for a number of years I decided to change course a little, and ex-band member Matt Beddoes helped me start a career teaching music as a private music tutor and a peripatetic music tutor. Here I begun working in various schools and music teaching academies across Worcestershire.





My music taste orignally stems from my parents, who were always listening to music as I was growing up. They listened to a variety of music including "The Grateful Dead", "The Cure", "Bob Dylan" and "The Stone Roses". As I got older I was gifted my first album from my Uncle, Sum 41's "All Killer, No Filler" and I bought my first album "Dookie" by Green Day, from here I began listening to more pop-punk such as "Blink-182", "Fall Out Boy", "Panic! At The Disco" etc.

As time went on, I discovered my first true favourite bands "We Are Scientists", "Ok Go" and "Motion City Soundtrack", this is where my music tastes began to shift to more Indie/Indie-Rock. I now started taking major influence from "The Pixies" and their dynamic range of heavy/soft chaotic style, while artists like "PAWS", "Mac Demarco" and "Shakey Graves" allowed me to appreciate embracing lo-fi as an aesthetic style in music.

Projects


Underground Ocean

"Underground Ocean" were an original alternative rock band based in Worcester that gigged across the UK.

Consisting of Stuart MacDonnell on rhythym guitar and vocals, Matt Beddoes on lead guitar, George Parker on bass and backing vocals and Lee Evans on drums, the band released an EP and three singles "Get Me Out", "I See Through You" and "Hide You".




The Mayor's Nest · Released Music

The Mayor's Nest

"The Mayor's Nest" is a chilled out indie duo based in Fernhill Heath, Worcester and gig solely in living rooms, to no audience.

Consisting of George Parker on guitar, keys, drums and Rebecca Parker on guitar ukulele and vocals, the husband and wife duo self-produce their music and so far has released a handful of covers including "Wicked Game" by Chris Isaak, "Ain't No Sunshine" by Bill Withers, "Everybody Wants to Rule the World" by Tears for Freas, etc.




django.

"django." is a lo-fi garage-rock / indie-rock project based in Worcester and will prospectively gig in the future

Written and produced by George Parker, "django." is a solo project which will involve Harvey Jones on bass guitar and Sebastian Baldwin on drums during live performances, so far, there has only been a demo EP, and a couple of singles released, but there is an album in the works which will be released eventually.
